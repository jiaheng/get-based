# Apple Health — brand assets

## Status
**INTENTIONALLY GENERIC.** `mark-mono.svg` is a generic file/document glyph — NOT Apple's pink heart.

## Why no Apple Health heart icon
Apple's HealthKit Marketing Guidelines explicitly restrict the Apple Health heart icon to apps that integrate via the HealthKit API. We do **file import** (the user manually exports `export.zip` from the iPhone Health app), which is not HealthKit integration. Using their heart on a Connect/Import button would imply HealthKit integration we don't have — that's a guideline violation and could trigger a takedown notice on the App Store ecosystem (and on our website).

## What's permitted
- Saying "Apple Health" as plain text — yes.
- Saying "Import your Apple Health export" — yes.
- The phrase "Works with Apple Health" — borderline, prefer "Reads exports from the Apple Health app".
- Apple's heart icon on our button — **NO**.
- "Powered by Apple Health" — **NO** (HealthKit-only phrase).

## Reference
- Apple HealthKit Marketing Guidelines: https://developer.apple.com/health-fitness/

## DO NOT replace the placeholder with Apple's heart icon during Phase 2b.
The generic file-import glyph is the correct permanent state for our use case.
